                        Tom In Jefferson • 7 years ago

                        First a minor correction in your article. Siskiyou County Board of Supervisors passed a resolution withdrawing from the state of California and supporting the formation of the State Of Jefferson. The June 3rd ballot measure was an attempt to have Siskiyou County become a territory by withdrawing from California and the US. The voters didn't approve the Territory idea. On to the real point I'd like to make. You stated there have been 220 attempts to split up the state and I've seen articles that claim there have been nearly 400 attempts, and these efforts have been going on almost since the treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo was signed and California became part of the US. As someone who grew up in Anaheim and now lives in Weed California and who has spent the last 10 years in local politics (for those of you in the City of Bell that means 100% unpaid volunteer Director) I can say the idea that something needs to change isn't hard to grasp. The only thing we have in common with Los Angeles and the other urban centers in California is we both like OUR water. Our representative in the state assembly has 11 counties in his district, the City of Santa Monica has 13 representatives. Our Senator has 9 counties in his district how many state senators does LA or San Francisco have? Point is in our representative government we in the North State (PS Jerry Brown per your recent comments LA and SF are political book ends not geographical book ends) have no representation. Arnold Schwarzenegger signed an agreement to remove 4 hydro-electric dams in our County, by any assessment it means economic disaster for our residents. Arnold never spoke to a single County representative before he signed the agreement in principal nor did he ever set foot in our County. Jerry Brown recently signed a compact with a local Tribe to build a Gambling Casino in our county. It's not supported by anyone other than the Tribe, doesn't matter Jerry signed the deal a senator from Santa Ana (Lou Correa) is carrying the confirmation legislation in the state senate and we just have to provide what ever service the Tribe desires. Finally, a state representative from the San Fernando Valley proposed and got legislation approved 3 years ago it's called AB129 the "State Responsibility Area" fire tax, or SRA Fee. It assesses every habitable structure (outside of incorporated cities) $150 per year (actually it has a cost of living escalator so it's higher now). The money goes int the states general fund none of it comes to the areas assessed nor is used to fight fires. Thanks Southern California you've really helped out a lot of folks living in rural areas who are already struggling to support their volunteer fire departments. We in the north state have our own set of challenges we need the freedom and a responsive government so we can enjoy the benefits of our great country and deal with the issues that impact us on a daily basis. Glad to hear that LA and SF are enjoying an economic rebirth, our economy isn't even on life support it's dead and it's been dead for 20 years. The reason there have been so many attempts at splitting up California is because it's a Good Idea. The Time Has Come For 51. It's our only hope if equal representation is to be more than a platitude.
