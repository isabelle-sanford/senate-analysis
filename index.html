Indroduction

The United States Senate is the upper chamber of the United States Congress, which, along with the House of Representatives - the lower house - constitutes the legislature of the United States. The Senate is composed of senators, each of whom represents a single state in its entirety. Each state is equally represented by two senators who serve staggered terms of six years. The Senate has unique advice and consent power such as ratified treaties, Confirmation of Cabinet secretaries, Confirmation of Supreme Court justices, Confirmation of federal judges, Confirmation of flag officers, regulators officials, ambassadors, federal uniformed officers, Conducts impeachment trials and Protects the rights of individual states (vs the House that represents individual districts). Due to the power and influences that the Senate has on the federal level, it is important to have fair representation in the Senate.

Suggestions for Senate Reform

    1. Grant statehood to DC and Puerto Rico
    • Combined population is almost 4 million and is largely of black and Hispanic demographic
    • Compare this to Wyoming who has 2 Senators, approximately 580,000 mostly white residents.
    • This would require a majority of vote from both houses in Congress

    2. Give each state 1 seat and apportion the rest based on population
    • Total US Population/100 then allocate Senators based on their share of the population

      • § 26 States: 1 Senator each
      • § 12 States: 2 Senators each

  3. Remove the filibuster and require only a simple majority and support of the majority leader
